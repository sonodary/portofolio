Effect of Driving Alone for Long Commutes on Poor Mental Health Outcomes (Grinnell College: Fall 2022)

  • In our research project, my group investigated the impact of long commutes on mental health using extra sums of squares analysis. We collected and analyzed data from the 2020 County Health Rankings dataset, which included 21 explanatory variables related to mental health outcomes. Through model analysis, we developed a reduced model with a noteworthy 76.2% adjusted R2 value, identifying significant predictors of poor mental health. Our findings suggested a potential connection between long commutes and diminished mental health when considering other factors. As the sole member with R coding expertise, I took the lead in data wrangling, algorithm implementation, and data visualization. Our efforts were recognized with an honorable mention in the Undergraduate Statistics Class Project (USCLAP) competition for our paper.

    • See the paper
